Consumers drive 70 per cent of US gross domestic product and they’ve been dealt multiple blows.
Already, there’s talk of pent-up demand as consumers dream about getting out of the house for more than groceries and prescriptions.
“There’s willingness to spend and there’s ability to spend,” said Jack Kleinhenz, chief economist for the National Federation of Retailers.
Among 26 countries surveyed since April 24, the average was 62 per cent, with only Finland below 40 per cent.
“This might take a long, long time for people to feel comfortable again,” he says.
