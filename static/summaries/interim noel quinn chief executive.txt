Mr Quinn’s “primary challenge will be to provide ongoing liquidity support to customers in tandem with government support schemes,” said John Cronin, an analyst at Goodbody Stockbrokers in Dublin.
Even before the stunning fallout from the virus paralysed economies around the world, HSBC chairman Mark Tucker’s chief executive search had grown fraught.
By sticking with Mr Quinn, Mr Tucker went with what insiders call a safe pair of hands who has impressed directors by acting decisively since John Flint was ousted in August.
Mr Quinn reorganised the executive ranks and last month unveiled a revamp that looked set to cut about 35,000 jobs and numerous underperforming businesses.
“It seemed that they were looking for external candidates,” said Eric Moore, portfolio manager at Miton Group in London referring to Mr Quinn’s appointment.
